## Overview

Functional equations ask you to determine all functions satisfying a condition.
Look for special inputs, symmetry, and invariants.

## Key Ideas

- Try small values like $0, 1, -1$ when allowed.
- Substitute to eliminate variables and express everything in terms of $f(0)$.
- If the equation is symmetric, try swapping variables to get new relations.

## Core Skills

### Normalize with Special Inputs

Plug in $0$, $1$, $-1$, $x$, $-x$, or $y=x$ to isolate constants and discover
parity or linearity.

### Build a Solution Family

Check constant and linear candidates first. Then verify directly in the
original equation.

### Use Symmetry and Swaps

If the equation is symmetric in $x$ and $y$, swapping can give extra equations
that eliminate terms.

## Worked Example

If $f(x+1)=f(x)+2x+1$ and $f(0)=0$, find $f(3)$.

Compute step by step: $f(1)=0+1$, $f(2)=1+3=4$, $f(3)=4+5=9$.
This matches $f(n)=n^2$ for nonnegative integers.

## More Examples

### Example 1: Odd or Even

If $f(x)+f(-x)=0$ for all $x$, what can you conclude?

$f$ is odd: $f(-x)=-f(x)$.

### Example 2: Linear Guess

Solve $f(x+y)=f(x)+f(y)+2$ for all real $x,y$.

Try $f(x)=ax+b$. Then $ax+ay+b = ax+b+ay+b+2$, so $b=-2$. Thus $f(x)=ax-2$.

### Example 3: Constant Check

Solve $f(x+y)=f(x)f(y)$ with $f(0)=0$.

Plugging $x=0$ gives $f(y)=f(0)f(y)=0$, so $f$ is identically zero.

## Strategy Checklist

- Plug in $0$, $x$, and $-x$ first.
- Test constant and linear candidates.
- Swap variables to generate new relations.
- Verify all candidates in the original equation.
