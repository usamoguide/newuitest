## Overview

SFFT is the reverse of FOIL. It helps factor expressions with four terms that
match a rectangle pattern.

## Key Ideas

- $xy + kx + jy + jk = (x+j)(y+k)$.
- Look for two pairs that share a common binomial.
- Works especially well in integer and Diophantine problems.

## Core Skills

### Group by Pairs

Reorder terms to group by $x$ and $y$ parts, then factor the shared binomial.

### Create a Rectangle

Think of the four terms as corners of a rectangle. The two side lengths become
the binomial factors.

### Scale First

If coefficients are large, factor out a gcd to simplify before applying SFFT.

## Worked Example

Factor $ab + 3a + 5b + 15$.

Group: $ab + 3a + 5b + 15 = a(b+3) + 5(b+3)$.
Factor the common binomial: $(a+5)(b+3)$.

## More Examples

### Example 1: Reorder Terms

Factor $6x + 2y + 3xy + 4$.

Reorder as $3xy + 6x + 2y + 4 = 3x(y+2) + 2(y+2) = (3x+2)(y+2)$.

### Example 2: Factor a GCD

Factor $12xy + 8x + 6y + 4$.

Factor out $2$: $2(6xy+4x+3y+2) = 2(3x+1)(2y+2)$.

### Example 3: Solve for Integers

Solve $xy + 4x + 3y = 18$ for integers $x,y$.

Rewrite as $(x+3)(y+4)=30$.

## Strategy Checklist

- Reorder terms to form two clean pairs.
- Factor out a gcd before grouping.
- Check by expanding your factors.

## Common Pitfalls

- Mixing the constants across the wrong variable.
- Missing the pairing when terms are not in order.
- Forgetting to factor out a gcd first.
