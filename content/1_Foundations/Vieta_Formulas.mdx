## Overview

Vieta's formulas turn sums and products of roots into coefficients. They are
indispensable for root-sum problems and symmetric expressions.

## Key Ideas

- For $ax^2+bx+c=0$ with roots $r,s$: $r+s=-\frac{b}{a}$, $rs=\frac{c}{a}$.
- For $x^3+px^2+qx+r=0$ with roots $\alpha,\beta,\gamma$:
  $\alpha+\beta+\gamma=-p$, $\alpha\beta+\alpha\gamma+\beta\gamma=q$,
  $\alpha\beta\gamma=-r$.
- Build a polynomial when the sum and product are known.

## Core Skills

### Normalize to Monic Form

If the quadratic is not monic, divide by $a$ first to read off coefficients.

### Build a Polynomial

If $r+s$ and $rs$ are known, the monic quadratic is
$x^2-(r+s)x+rs$.

### Use Symmetric Expressions

Expressions like $r^2+s^2$ or $1/r+1/s$ can be rewritten using $r+s$ and $rs$.

## Worked Example

Find a quadratic with roots $3$ and $5$.

If $r+s=8$ and $rs=15$, then the monic quadratic is
$x^2-(r+s)x+rs = x^2-8x+15$.

## More Examples

### Example 1: Sum and Product

If a quadratic is $2x^2-3x-5=0$, find the sum and product of its roots.

$r+s=3/2$ and $rs=-5/2$.

### Example 2: Symmetric Expression

If $r+s=4$ and $rs=1$, find $r^2+s^2$.

$r^2+s^2=(r+s)^2-2rs=16-2=14$.

### Example 3: Reciprocal Sum

If roots are $r,s$ with $r+s=5$ and $rs=6$, find $\frac{1}{r}+\frac{1}{s}$.

$\frac{1}{r}+\frac{1}{s}=\frac{r+s}{rs}=5/6$.

## Strategy Checklist

- Divide by $a$ to get monic form.
- Use $r+s$ and $rs$ to rewrite symmetric expressions.
- Build a polynomial when roots or sums are given.

## Common Pitfalls

- Forgetting the sign on the coefficient of $x$.
- Mixing the monic and non-monic forms.
- Confusing $r+s$ with $rs$ in symmetric expressions.
