## Overview

Exponent rules let you rewrite expressions quickly without expanding. Mastery
here makes later factoring and functional equations much easier.

## Key Ideas

- $x^a x^b = x^{a+b}$ and $\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}$.
- $(x^a)^b = x^{ab}$ and $(xy)^a = x^a y^a$.
- $x^{-a} = \frac{1}{x^a}$ for $x \ne 0$.
- $x^0 = 1$ for $x \ne 0$.

## Core Skills

### Combine Like Bases

Only add or subtract exponents when the bases are the same.

### Handle Negative and Fractional Exponents

Move negative exponents to the denominator and rewrite fractional exponents as
roots when needed.

### Avoid Distributing Over Sums

Remember $(x+y)^a$ does not split into $x^a+y^a$.

## Worked Example

Simplify $\frac{(2x^3y^{-2})^2}{4x^{-1}y}$.

First expand the power: $(2x^3y^{-2})^2 = 4x^6y^{-4}$.
Then divide: $\frac{4x^6y^{-4}}{4x^{-1}y} = x^{6-(-1)} y^{-4-1} = x^7 y^{-5}$.
So the expression is $\frac{x^7}{y^5}$.

## More Examples

### Example 1: Negative Exponents

Simplify $x^{-3}y^2/x^{-1}$.

$x^{-3}/x^{-1}=x^{-2}$, so the result is $\frac{y^2}{x^2}$.

### Example 2: Fractional Exponents

Simplify $(16x^4)^{1/2}$.

$4x^2$ (assuming $x\ge 0$).

### Example 3: Combine Bases

Simplify $3^2\cdot 3^5$.

$3^7$.

## Strategy Checklist

- Combine exponents only for matching bases.
- Move negative exponents across the fraction bar.
- Convert fractional exponents to roots if helpful.
- Check domain restrictions when using even roots.

## Common Pitfalls

- Forgetting that negative exponents move factors to the denominator.
- Distributing powers across sums, e.g., $(x+y)^2 \ne x^2 + y^2$.
- Canceling terms with different bases.
